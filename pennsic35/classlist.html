Danse de Cleves, Beaulte de Castille, one or two others as time permits. Countess Mara Kolarova 1
Fri 8/11 12:00PM Early Progressive Country Dances These are the kinds of progressive dances that they did long before Hole in the Wall and La Matelotte (The Hornpipe or Female Sailor): Up Tails All, Once I Loved a Maiden Faire, & My Lady Cullen. Lord Henry of Maldon 2

This class will cover many of the typical formations and figures, and several currently popular dances. Lord Henry of Maldon 1

Sat 8/12 10:00AM Intermediate English Country Dancing Because you can never know too many Playford dances! Tentatively, this class will cover Jack Pudding, The Merry Merry Milk Maids, If All the World Were Paper, and Newcastle. Lord Henry of Maldon 2
Sat 8/12 12:00PM Bransles and Basse Danses from Arena Arena published a dance manual for the law-students of Avignon in the 1520s. Apparently they needed it: he felt that he needed to tell them not to offer to a girl a hand they'd just sneezed into. The Basse Danse and the bransles Arena described will be covered—similar to Orchesographie but in some ways quite different. Messer Sion Andreas o Wynedd 1
Sat 8/12 1:00PM English Revels Prep Practice for this evening's English Revels: English measures and country dances, and a few French brawls, all reconstructed by the teacher. Lord Henry of Maldon 2
Sat 8/12 3:00PM Focused Beginner Class—15th Century Folk Dance: Saltarello and Piva This class will cover the culture and practice of two of the most popular improvised dance forms of not only Italy, but of Spain and France. Part lecture, part practicum. No choreographies to remember, just pure, simple fun. THL Judith de Northumbria 1
Sat 8/12 4:00PM Pennsic Ball Prep Learn the dances that will be done at the Pennsic Ball. The first of the class will focus specifically on the easier dances, while the second of class will concentrate on more challenging ones. THL Judith de Northumbria 2
Sun 8/13 10:00AM Intro to English Country Dance This class teaches the basics of English Country Dance, using several of the most common dances. Lord Lorenzo Petrucci 1
Sun 8/13 11:00AM Branles for All Branles are an easy and fun type of dance described by French dancemaster Arbeau. We will discuss branle technique and dance a number of branles, both common and obscure. Suitable for everyone from beginners to advanced dancers. Lady Katherine Mercer 1
Sun 8/13 12:00PM Menages a Deux: La Fia Guilmina This is a beautiful 15th c. piece for two that will allow you and a (hopefully likable) partner to display grace and passion on the floor. All steps will be taught, but this class is aimed at experienced dancers. THL Judith de Northumbria 1
Sun 8/13 1:00PM Low, Slow, and Flirtatious: Gioioso Spagnuolo The title says it all. Some like it hot--- and some like their bassadanze Spanish hot, as is the case in this dance whose title means `Joy in the Spanish style'. For beginners as well as experienced dancers. All steps will be taught, and ornamentation and styling briefly discussed. THL Judith de Northumbria 1
Sun 8/13 2:00PM Ballo del Fiore for 2 and 3 Ballo del Fiore is a very commonly done, easy mixer dance for couples from 16th century Italy. However, Caroso also wrote down a special choreography for three dancers. Both will be taught. Lady Lynnette 1
Sun 8/13 3:00PM Focused Beginner Class--Galliards Galliards don't have to be intimidating just because they're improvised. In this class we'll go over the basic galliard and a few variations. Then we'll learn how to string the pieces together into a full galliard sequence with a partner. Lady Katherine Mercer 1
Mon 8/14 10:00AM English Country Dances for 8 (Newcastle and Goddesses) Newcastle is a little complex and Goddesses is rollicking, but both are lots of fun. Good for advanced beginners to intermediate. Master Ocatvio de Flores 1
Mon 8/14 11:00AM Bizarria d'Amore and Gracca Amorosa Two very popular, intermediate level dances from Caroso. Lord Darius the Dancer 1
Mon 8/14 12:00PM Lombardy Longways: Colonesse Yes, the Italian peninsula enjoyed longways set dances in the 15th c., with hays and all of the fun that an English dance has, and more! Some dance experience recommended, but all steps will be taught. THL Judith de Northumbria 1
Mon 8/14 1:00PM Low, Slow, and Flirtatious: Lauro The title says it all. Come learn this gem of a dance composed by none other than Lorenzo de' Medici, il Magnifico. A perfect way to hone your dancing skills for the Pennsic Ball. For beginners as well as experienced dancers; all steps will be taught, and ornamentation and styling briefly discussed. THL Judith de Northumbria 1
Mon 8/14 2:00PM The Joy of Watching Dance The secret pleasures of watching dance. How to encourage others, enjoy the stuff you don't know, and generally have a ball. Lady Magnunnr Hringsdottir 1

Familiarity with 16th century Italian steps is helpful for this intermediate/advanced level class. THL Alina of Foxwood 1
Tue 8/15 11:00AM Romantic Dances for 2 (Danse de Cleves, Bouree Valsee) Danse de Cleves taught from the period manuscript. Bouree Valsee is an active dance, conjectured by Mistress Silvija the Landlady. Master Octavio de Flores 1
Tue 8/15 12:00PM Renaissance Folk Dance: Chirintana This class will cover a widely (and enduringly) popular dance that seems itself to exhibit several of the elements of later longways English Country Dances, namely the high-energy hays in such dances as La Trenchmore. For complete beginners as well as experienced dancers. THL Judith de Northumbria 1
Tue 8/15 1:00PM Low, Slow, and Flirtatious: Cupido The title says it all. How can one go wrong with a dance entitled `Cupid'? A short, sweet piece that will afford you plenty of partner contact. All steps will be taught, but dance experience is recommended. Ornamentation and styling briefly discussed. THL Judith de Northumbria 1
Tue 8/15 2:00PM My Lady Cullen and the Friar and the Nun Two progression dances from 1st edition Playford will be taught. Lord Jody Brytan and THL Alina of Foxwood 1
Tue 8/15 3:00PM Focused Beginner Class—English Country Dance This class is geared toward the individual. Learn the basic elements of English Country Dancing, according to Playford, then name the dances you'd like to learn, practice, or just dance for fun. Saltatoris Dance Guild 1

Wed 8/16 10:00PM Il Bianco Fiore A 16th Century Italian dance by Cesare Negri for 2 couples. If you like Bizzaria d'Amore, Il Villanicco, and/or Lo Spagnoletto, you'll find this dance delightfully familiar. If you're unfamiliar with Negri's dances, this dance will provides you with many of the step sequences found in other commonly done Negri dances. Familiarity with 16th century Italian steps is helpful for this intermediate/advanced level class. THL Alina of Foxwood 1
Wed 8/16 11:00AM Voltati in ça Rosina 15th century Italian dance for 1 man and 2 women or vice versa. If there is time, Marchesana and/or Leoncello will also be taught. Lord Lorenzo Petrucci 1
Wed 8/16 12:00PM Lombardy Longways: Pizochara Yes, the Italians had longways dances too, with hays and all of the fun that an English dance has, and more! Culture, dance and manners also discussed. Some dance experience recommended, but all steps will be taught. THL Judith de Northumbria 1
Wed 8/16 1:00PM Intermediate English Country Dance The dances taught will depend on the number of participants and the dances they wish to learn. Possibilities include Hyde Park, If All the World Were Paper, Whirlygig, Parsons Farewell, and Picking Up Sticks. Saltatoris Dance Guild 2
Wed 8/16 3:00PM Focused Beginner Class - Bransles Bransles are some of the earliest done dances in the SCA. Focus will be a common suite of them - Aridan, Cassandra, Charlotte, Pinagay, and War. Lord Darius the Dancer 1
Wed 8/16 4:00PM Contrapasso and Contentezza We will learn 16th century Italian dancemaster Caroso's Contrapasso in Due and Contentezza d'Amore. Focus will not be on the exact performance steps. Learning the basics of each step and how it relates to the choreography within the music will be encouraged. No prior dance experience, no partner required. Designed for beginners, those who just like to dance or need a refresher are more than welcome to join. Master Phillip White 1
Wed 8/16 5:00PM French Galliard Steps and Technique Beginning with basic techniques, we will be working on fancy footwork and showy leaps as reconstructed by the teacher based on Arbeau. The class might go at a moderate pace if needed to accommodate Pennsic footsoreness. Lord Henry of Maldon 1

Thu 8/17 12:00PM Playing with Your Partner So, you have the basics of the dances down; now come learn some ways to spice up your dancing--- and how different music can affect your movement. Exemplar dances will be Rosti Boli Gioioso and Amoroso. Students should be familiar with these two dances. THL Judith de Northumbria 1

Thu 8/17 4:00PM Obscure Orchesography Everyone knows Horses' Branle and the Belle Que Pavan, but can you coronto? In this class we'll cover some of the more obscure dances described in Arbeau's 1589 dance manual Orchesography in preparation for the Arbeau Party. Lady Katherine Mercer 1
